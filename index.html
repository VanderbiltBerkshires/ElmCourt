Business Overview

Elm Court is an 89-acre historic Gilded Age estate, the largest shingled residence in the U.S., owned by Vanderbilt Berkshires Estate, LLC. Originally a summer residence for Emily T. Vanderbilt, the property is being restored with a commitment to its legendary past and prominence in the Lenox and Stockbridge communities.

Elm Court Amendment: Preserving Tomorrow

On December 4, we're seeking a targeted update to our special permit—ensuring 72% of the 89-acre estate remains forever protected as Olmsted-inspired landscapes, vital wetlands, and the serene Lilly Pond, while enabling modest evolution on 28% (custom sites, low-scale cottages). This honors Emily Vanderbilt Sloane's vision: a living legacy, not a lost relic.

Spotlight: Restoring the Historic Manor House

The 1886 Peabody & Stearns masterpiece—America's largest shingled residence—is being meticulously revived with original materials and spatial integrity, creating 26 immersive guest suites. No additions, just authentic renewal to keep its Gilded Age heart beating for generations.

Echoes of the Past: The Vanderbilt Vision That Shaped Elm Court

Imagine 1886: The air hums with the promise of endless summers. Emily Vanderbilt Sloane, niece of the legendary Cornelius Vanderbilt, commissions architects Peabody & Stearns to craft a shingle-style masterpiece—a 55,000-square-foot Manor House blending rustic charm with unparalleled luxury. Flanked by Frederick Law Olmsted's masterful landscape design, the estate unfolds like a living tapestry: rolling lawns, whispering woodlands, and the serene Lilly Pond, a haven for family gatherings, lavish balls, and quiet reflection.

For over a century, Elm Court has whispered tales of innovation and indulgence—the Vanderbilts' bold fusion of nature and architecture that defined America's Gilded Age. It was a sanctuary where fortunes were made in conversation, and legacies were etched into the Berkshires' soul. Today, we honor that heritage not as a relic, but as the foundation for tomorrow.

Forward to Tomorrow: Preservation Meets Purposeful Progress

What if the future could feel like coming home? At Vanderbilt Berkshires Estate, we're channeling the Vanderbilts' pioneering ethos to create a destination that's 72% devoted to timeless preservation and just 28% to thoughtful evolution. This balanced blueprint safeguards Elm Court's heart while unlocking its potential as a world-class retreat.

  • The Manor House Reborn: Step through restored grand halls into 26 bespoke hotel keys—each a nod to Vanderbilt-era splendor, with modern comforts like spa-inspired baths and panoramic views.
  • Olmsted's Enduring Canvas: Over 70 acres remain untouched homage to Frederick Law Olmsted's genius—meandering paths, native wildflowers, and sculpted vistas that invite leisurely strolls and al fresco weddings.
  • Guardians of the Wild: Wetlands and the abutting Lilly Pond—vital ecosystems teeming with biodiversity—are forever protected.

The New Horizon: Innovation Rooted in Legacy

Daring like the Vanderbilts before us, we're dedicating 28% of the estate to forward-thinking development that enhances, never overshadows:

  • Custom Home Sites: Exquisite parcels for visionary homeowners, designed to blend seamlessly with the landscape.
  • 12 Luxe 4-Plex Cottages: Delivering 48 additional hotel keys in charming clusters, evoking Elm Court's storied guest houses.

This measured expansion isn't growth for growth's sake; it's a bridge to accessibility, inviting a new generation to create their own Vanderbilt memories.
